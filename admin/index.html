🗣 Conversation Guide

Opening
"Hey, quick question — when a customer wants to order from you but you're slammed, what do they do? Wait? Leave? Call back? What if they could just order from their phone and you'd see it the second it came in?"
The Hook
"We built this for food trucks and restaurants right here in Salado. Every order shows up on a screen your staff watches in real time. No missed orders. No 'sorry we're out.' Customers get a text when it's ready."
The Value
"Think about a Friday night — how many people walked past, looked at your line, and kept walking? Those are orders you never got. Online ordering captures them before they even leave home."
The Ask
"You don't pay anything upfront. No monthly fees, no contracts. We only make money when you do. Want me to show you what it looks like? Takes 60 seconds on my phone."

🛡 Common Objections

  • "We already take orders over the phone."
    "That's great — this doesn't replace that. It just captures the people who won't call. A lot of people, especially younger customers, would rather tap their phone than make a call."
  • "We're too busy to deal with new tech."
    "That's exactly why this works — you don't set anything up. Orders just show up on a screen. Your staff doesn't change what they do."
  • "We don't really need a website."
    "Totally fair. The website is just how customers find the ordering page. What they actually care about is not waiting in line."
  • "What's the catch?"
    "There's no catch. We only charge when an order goes through — 2 cents on every dollar. If you never use it, you never pay a thing."
  • "Can I think about it?"
    "Of course. Can I leave you the website so you can look at a live example? It's already running for a few businesses here in Salado."
